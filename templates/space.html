Problem Statement 1:
Space exploration missions often face communication delays due to vast distances, making it difficult to make real-time decisions. How can we develop an autonomous decision- making system for space missions that can operate without waiting for communication from Earth?
Problem Statement 2:
As space exploration advances, managing the increasing amount of space debris has become a critical challenge. Space debris poses significant risks to satellites, spacecraft, and the future of space missions, with current tracking and mitigation efforts often being insufficient. How can we develop real-time solutions to track, manage, and remove space debris to ensure the safety and sustainability of space exploration?
Problem Statement 3:
As space stations become crucial hubs for long-term space missions, ensuring the safety, health, and efficiency of astronauts living in space is a growing challenge. Limited resources, space, and communication delays make it difficult to manage life-support systems and daily operations in real-time. How can we create innovative, real-time solutions to monitor and optimize life support, health, and operations aboard space stations, ensuring astronauts' safety and mission success?
