Agriculture

Problem Statement 1:
With growing environmental uncertainties and increasing demand for sustainable food production, how can we design an intelligent, self-regulating cultivation system that seamlessly adapts to real-time environmental changes, optimizes resource allocation, and ensures consistent, high-quality yields—without relying on traditional agricultural methods.
Problem Statement 2:
Water scarcity in agriculture leads to inefficient irrigation, causing water waste and lower crop yields. Traditional methods often result in over or under-watering. How can we develop a smart irrigation system using sensors and IoT technology to monitor soil moisture and weather conditions, providing farmers with real-time data to optimize water usage and enhance crop yields.
Problem Statement 3:
There are multiple vegetable markets in various areas, and each farmer or vendor can only visit one market at a time. They must sell their vegetables at whatever price is offered, regardless of whether it’s a profit or loss. The goal is to develop a web or any application that displays real-time pricing information for vegetables at each market, helping farmers make informed decisions about where to sell their produce based on the current prices.
